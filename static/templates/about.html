The Prodigal About Page

The TL;DR Version (2.0)

Bitbanter lets you submit original, creative articles (even anonymously) that others can tip through bitcoins (yes, even anonymously). The higher the tips, the more prominent your article. 80% of your tips are transferred to you. The other 20% keeps me incentivized to keep Bitbanter rolling and prevents gaming through self-promotion. New with v2: more seamless support for microtransactions through deeper Coinbase integration. Okay, so what is this thing?

Technically speaking, it's a Coinbase-powered web app designed for speed, simplicity, and random acts of humor. To use my favorite startup metaphor trope: "Bitbanter is the bitcoin-addicted, ambiguously-fathered lovechild orphaned after a tawdry threesome between reddit, McSweenys, and The Onion."

...let's approach this from another angle. Bitbanter is my attempt at a decentralized Comedian's Stone; like a Philosopher's Stone, but instead of transforming base metals into pure gold, the Comedian's Stone turns base humor into pure value ("that's comedy gold, Jerry!"). Consider this diagram:

To me, the Internet has always been about making these arrows more efficient -- reducing barriers, not just for humorists/consumers, but for all creators/consumers -- and Bitbanter is my humble/awesome experiment to that end.

From a more self-motivated perspective: I wanted a place to write satire pseudonymously, to give people some laughs, and to convert some of those laughs into sweet, sweet satoshis...all while promoting the content that generated the most laughs. A platform like that didn't exist, so I made one.

We're living in an era filled with cool shit, like decentralized crypto-currencies, rapid-deployment web frameworks, electric cars, and polar bear fecal transplants. Bitbanter owes its existence to that cool shit. Especially that polar bear fecal transplant, which was a goddamn lifesaver.

How long until you start showing ads?

To me, reducing the barriers between creators/consumers includes telling ads to bugger off. In addition to being uninspired, that model typically distort the incentives of both content producers and consumers. Though in some important cases, like Google search/local, things can line up nicely for everyone involved. ::Obligatory golf clap to the Google Overlords::

Full disclosure: I am running some analytics on the site (to help me understand what works and what doesn't), but other that I only collect whatever people voluntarily submit with their articles. And even a user's Coinbase email address is hidden from other users (it's stored on the server). As long as I'm around, Bitbanter will never descend into selling ads and/or data territory.

Great, how does it work?

When you first visit Bitbanter, you'll see a list of articles with bitcoin values. Those bitcoin values are the total amount of tips granted by other users. The more tips, the higher the article. Click on an article's headline to see more. To tip an author for a chuckle (or promote your own work, you cheeky bastard), click on the bitcoin amount and watch as Coinbase magically takes care of the rest (and yes, you can tip really, really, really small amounts). Your tip will immediately add to the article's total, but will take one hour until it's disbursed to the author. 80% of your bitcoins go to the author and 20% will go to Bitbanter (suck it, cheeky self-promoters!).

You can submit an article by clicking on the "+" in the header. You're only required to submit a headline, a short body and your Coinbase email address. After submitting an article, Bitbanter will display articles in descending order based on submission date to confirm that your article was added.

Click on the Bitbanter title to change article ordering between "most recent" and "highest tips." The main view only shows articles that have been submitted in the last 30 days, but old articles are always accessible (and tippable!) through their link. And that's it!

Why bitcoins?

They reduce payment friction, they're transnational, they support micropayments (a halfpenny for a laugh!) and they enable both anonymous content and tipping. If you choose, both Bitbanter's readers and I will never know who you are, but you'll get to write what you want (without revealing your identity, like me) and you can still get credit (through bitcoins and your Twitter handle).

Your bitcoin article-listing algorithm is stupidly naive and will break easily.

Yes, it's stupidly naive and will break easily, just like your mother.

But look at it this way: as long as Bitbanter remains small, the algorithm can be dumb. Bitcoin tips are less liquid than up/downvoting, but does it matter? Tips will highlight good submissions, just as (paid) reddit gold highlights good comments. And if someone pays themselves to promote their work, well, that's their prerogative, but because Bitbanter keeps 20% it's not exactly a free activity for them.

That said, I reserve the right to be completely wrong. If your mother is a close approximation for my dead-simple algorithm, then the algorithm -- unlike your dead and simple mother -- is easily adjusted. Examples of potential changes: changing the time period for the article list, ordering by the median tip instead of total tips, allowing anonymous downvotes, etc.
